How to make it!

  • Spaghetti: 400g
  • Pancetta: 150g, diced (or guanciale if you can find it)
  • Eggs: 3 large
  • Pecorino Romano Cheese: 50g, finely grated
  • Parmesan Cheese: 50g, finely grated
  • Garlic: 2 cloves, peeled and left whole
  • Black Pepper: Freshly ground
  • Salt: For pasta water

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    • Finely grate the Pecorino Romano and Parmesan cheese and mix them together. Beat the eggs in a medium bowl and season with a little freshly ground black pepper. Set aside.
  2. Cook the Pancetta:
    • Heat a large frying pan over medium heat. Add the pancetta and cook until it becomes crispy. Remove the garlic once it has imparted its flavor and discard it.
  3. Cook the Spaghetti:
    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente, according to the package instructions. Reserve a cup of pasta water before draining the spaghetti.
  4. Combine Everything:
    • Remove the frying pan with the pancetta from the heat. Add the drained spaghetti to the pan and toss to combine. Quickly pour in the beaten eggs and cheese mixture, stirring vigorously to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time to achieve the desired consistency.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve immediately with a sprinkling of the remaining cheese and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper.
